UCU CARE PROJECT

The Center for Student Leadership and Development-Character and Values Formation Unit at Urdaneta City University launched an inspiring initiative entitled "UCUIANS CARE PROJECT," with the theme "Caring Beyond Borders! Healthy Student, Healthy Mind." Aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 1 — No Poverty, this project seeks to promote the values of empathy, generosity, and community-mindedness by encouraging students to practice the timeless principle that caring is sharing. The UCUIANS CARE PROJECT is designed to build awareness of the importance of sharing and compassion, fostering a campus environment where students are encouraged to think beyond themselves. Through this initiative, participants gain insight into the significance of generosity as a catalyst for personal and communal growth. Such acts of kindness help reinforce strong bonds within the university community, allowing individuals to relate to one another with understanding and respect.

Rooted in the Filipino tradition of Bayanihan, or communal unity, the project embodies the collective spirit that uplifts the less fortunate. This solidarity, preserved in our society's moral fabric, reminds students of their ability and responsibility to make a difference. The first implementation of the program took place on August 13, 2023, at the CSLD Square, where fifty student beneficiaries from various colleges within the university gathered to participate in activities centered around support, empowerment, and wellness. By engaging in this initiative, students take their first steps toward building a more compassionate, equitable future—one where everyone thrives, no matter their background or circumstances.

AFFORDABLE EDUCATION FOR THE BRIGHT FUTURE OF UCU STUDENTS

Urdaneta City University (UCU), through its Center for Student Leadership and Development (CSLD), continues to demonstrate its dedication to fostering an inclusive and equitable learning environment by offering financial assistance through its Tuition Discount Program. This initiative, which aligns with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 1 No Poverty, is designed to provide essential support to students from economically disadvantaged families, helping reduce the barriers to education and empowering them to achieve their academic goals.

In its ongoing effort to break the cycle of poverty through education, UCU understands the significant impact that financial constraints can have on a student's academic journey. The Tuition Discount Program seeks to alleviate this burden by offering qualifying students reduced tuition fees, allowing them to focus on their studies and personal development without the constant worry of financial hardship.

The program aims to create an inclusive and supportive learning environment where students can thrive, regardless of their socio-economic background. By offering this assistance, UCU strengthens its mission of making quality education accessible to all, particularly those who need it the most.

In line with the university's broader vision, this initiative not only seeks to address immediate financial concerns but also plays a crucial role in shaping a more compassionate and empowered student community. UCU recognizes that education is a key factor in creating opportunities and breaking down barriers for those from low-income families, and through the Tuition Discount Program, the university is helping pave the way for a brighter future for its students.

By prioritizing inclusivity and accessibility, Urdaneta City University is taking a significant step toward addressing one of the most pressing challenges facing the global community today: poverty. With its ongoing support for financially disadvantaged students, UCU is contributing to the creation of a more equitable society where education serves as a powerful tool for social mobility and positive change.

EXTENSION ACTIVITY: CAS AGAPE

The College of Arts and Sciences, under the leadership of Dean Princess Quintana, organized the EXTENSION ACTIVITY: CAS AGAPE for the students of Tiposu Elementary School on December 15, 2023. This event, which aimed to provide both material and emotional support, was made possible through the collective efforts of the college's faculty and students, who came together to uplift and bring joy to the children in the community.

In terms of objectives, the EXTENSION ACTIVITY: CAS AGAPE Program aimed to accomplish several key goals. Firstly, it focused on providing essential school supplies to underprivileged students, ensuring that they had the necessary tools for their education. This effort helped ease the financial burden on families who might otherwise struggle to afford these basic items. Secondly, the program sought to offer hope and encouragement to children from economically disadvantaged backgrounds, particularly during the holiday season. By providing gifts and support, it aimed to lift their spirits and remind them that they are valued. Finally, the initiative sought to instill a sense of social responsibility in the faculty and students of the College of Arts and Sciences. By engaging in this community outreach, the participants not only helped the children but also gained a deeper appreciation for the importance of service and giving back to the community.

As a result of these combined efforts, the EXTENSION ACTIVITY: CAS AGAPE at Tiposu Elementary School was a tremendous success. Not only did it provide essential school supplies to students in need, but it also brought joy and encouragement to help them stay motivated in their studies. Under the leadership of Dean Princess Quintana, the College of Arts and Sciences reaffirmed its dedication to social responsibility and community engagement, emphasizing the importance of supporting others, especially during the holiday season.

In conclusion, the event highlighted how even small acts of kindness can have a significant impact on the lives of others. Looking forward, the College of Arts and Sciences remains committed to continuing such outreach initiatives, striving to uplift and support the most vulnerable members of the community.

CAMPUS STUDENT ORGANIZATIONS’ TRADE FAIR

Urdaneta City University (UCU) demonstrates its commitment to supporting students and the local community in building financially and socially sustainable businesses through the Center for Student Leadership and Development (CSLD). One of the core mandates of the CSLD is to create economic and entrepreneurial programs designed to empower students with the practical knowledge and skills essential for navigating the world of business and trade. This initiative not only helps students acquire a foundational understanding of entrepreneurship but also equips them to manage and generate their own financial resources—resources that can significantly enhance their academic and co-curricular experiences.

To fulfill this mandate, the CSLD has launched a pilot project for the Campus Student Organizations' Trade Fair, an innovative approach aimed at encouraging campus-based entrepreneurial ventures. This fair provides accredited Campus Student Organizations (CSOs) with a platform to showcase their products and services, gaining hands-on experience in running a business. By participating in this Trade Fair, CSOs are empowered to generate income that can fund their future activities, enabling them to carry out meaningful programs aligned with their vision, mission, goals, and objectives (VMGOs).

Beyond creating financial opportunities for students, this initiative supports the university's larger goal of fostering a supportive campus community. The trade fair and other entrepreneurial programs initiated by the CSLD connect students with resources, such as mentorship programs, training workshops, and access to university facilities. Through these resources, UCU not only facilitates the immediate needs of student organizations but also prepares students to launch and sustain their own ventures beyond their university experience, bridging the gap between academia and real-world entrepreneurship.

By providing platforms like the Trade Fair and other entrepreneurial support, UCU exemplifies how a university can play an integral role in community development and in cultivating students' potential as future business leaders. This program illustrates UCU's dedication to fostering an environment that makes student life enjoyable, fulfilling, and meaningful, empowering students to contribute economically and socially within their communities.

Urdaneta City University Teams Up with Schools and Barangays to Fight Poverty Through Extension Program

Urdaneta City University (UCU) has officially launched a series of extension programs aimed at addressing poverty in the region. This new initiative, which focuses on empowering local communities through educational support, is a collaborative effort between UCU, local schools, and barangays (villages). The program is part of the university's commitment to supporting the local government's ongoing efforts to eradicate poverty and contribute to the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goal of ending poverty in all its forms by 2030. In this way, UCU is reinforcing its role as a key partner in the fight against poverty, aligned with both local and global goals.

The official launch of the program was marked by the signing of a Memorandum of Agreement led by UCU's Extension Director, Jerry Dichoso, who played a pivotal role in forging partnerships between the university, local schools, and barangays. The event, held at the respective campuses of the partner schools and barangays, saw the signing of Memoranda of Agreement (MOAs) with several barangay councils and schools in the region, formalizing their commitment to work together in delivering the program's objectives. As a result, the partnerships were solidified, creating a clear framework for collaboration and ensuring that the program would reach its intended beneficiaries.

Furthermore, the partnership signing marked the beginning of a strong and meaningful collaboration that brought much-needed support to local communities. "Through this collective effort, we aimed to create a ripple effect that would empower local residents, enhance their skills, and provide them with the knowledge they needed to improve their lives and break free from the cycle of poverty," said Dichoso. "This was more than just a project; it was a movement for positive change." Thus, the initiative goes beyond immediate assistance, fostering long-term empowerment and self-sufficiency within the community.

In addition, the partnership between UCU, local schools, barangays, and the local government is an example of how education can be leveraged as a powerful tool for social change. UCU's extension program is a concrete step toward achieving a poverty-free future, aligning with the Philippine government's national development goals and the global mandate to eradicate poverty. By working together, these entities are amplifying the impact of the program and contributing to broader social transformation efforts. Looking ahead, UCU plans to expand the program to include additional barangays and schools, reaching even more marginalized communities. The university is also exploring opportunities to collaborate with NGOs, business organizations, and other stakeholders to enhance the impact of the program. As a result, the university is laying the groundwork for a sustained and far-reaching effort that will continue to benefit local communities for years to come.

GROWING OPPORTUNITIES: UCU LAUNCHES LIVELIHOOD TRAINING ON MUSHROOM FARMING

Urdaneta City University (UCU) is taking significant steps to support sustainable business initiatives with the launch of a Livelihood Training Program on Mushroom Production, held on September 15, 2019. The program, organized by the Center for Community Development and Extension Services (CCDES) under the leadership of Dr. Mary Ruth Cinchez, aims to provide participants with the practical skills needed to start their own mushroom farming businesses while promoting sustainable agricultural practices. In partnership with the Provincial Agriculture Office, represented by Mdm. Concordia S. Lee, the training focused on both the technical aspects of mushroom production and the entrepreneurial strategies needed to establish a viable, long-term business. Mdm. Lee, who served as the lead trainer, guided the participants through key processes such as cultivation, harvesting, and the economic potential of mushroom farming as a sustainable business venture.

The event was held at UCU's Audio Visual Room and saw strong participation from various members of the university community, including Deans, Department Heads, faculty, and students. The program is part of UCU's broader vision to empower individuals with the skills and knowledge needed to launch start-up businesses that contribute to the local economy and promote environmental sustainability.

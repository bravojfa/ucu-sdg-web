Webinar on “Heal the Unseen Battle: Reducing the Stigma and Psychological Misconceptions”

Urdaneta City University (UCU) fourth-year psychology students led an impactful virtual event titled “Heal the Unseen Battle: Reducing the Stigma and Psychological Misconceptions.” The day-long webinar, organized by students from UCU’s College of Human Sciences, Department of Psychology, aimed to address mental health misconceptions and reduce the stigma surrounding psychological issues. Streamed on Google Meet and Facebook Live, the event was attended by UCU students, employees, and participants from other universities, all united by a shared commitment to learning more about mental wellness.

The webinar addressed key aspects of mental health awareness, including the importance of dispelling common myths that contribute to misunderstanding and stigma. Expert speakers tackled pervasive misconceptions about mental health, providing participants with accurate information about mental illness, emotional resilience, and stress management. Discussions also highlighted how stigma often prevents individuals from seeking help, with speakers encouraging a community-wide approach to foster support and understanding around mental health concerns. Psychology students and faculty facilitated open conversations, creating a safe and respectful environment for attendees to share their own experiences and concerns.

Badang: Panagtaripatto Kadagiti Agtutubo (PTO) Project Empowers Youth Through Comprehensive Training and Support

The Badang: Panagtaripatto Kadagiti Agtutubo (PTO) project at Urdaneta City University (UCU) has been making a significant impact in the lives of its beneficiaries by providing essential training and educational opportunities aimed at building community service awareness and self-growth. The project, which continues to evolve through weekly learning sessions, has been a collaborative effort involving various colleges and departments, each contributing unique activities designed to empower youth and prepare them for future success.

One of the key components of the PTO project is the series of extension activities and workshops led by different departments, each focusing on the development of vital life skills. The College of Community Development and Extension Services (CCDES) conducted sessions that encouraged beneficiaries to reflect on their learning experiences, particularly in the areas of self-awareness and human relations. A Client Needs Assessment was also carried out to ensure that future activities would continue to meet the evolving needs of the participants.

University Policy for Equitable Access to Outreach Programs and Extension Activities

The University is committed to fostering a diverse, inclusive, and equitable environment in all of its outreach programs and extension activities. The University recognizes the importance of providing equal opportunities for all individuals, regardless of their ethnicity, religion, disability, immigration status, gender, or any other characteristic.

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that all individuals have equitable access to the University’s outreach programs and extension activities.This policy applies to all outreach programs, extension activities, and community engagement initiatives organized by the University.

Your Research Hub: Discover Thousands of Books & Multimedia at UCU Orata Library

Named in honor of Dr. Pedro T. Orata, a visionary in education, the UCU Orata Library serves as a cornerstone of learning and exploration for students, faculty, and the entire community. Three-day onsite workshop on “Research Writing: Statistical Data Analysis, Results and Discussion, Conclusion, and Recommendation”

Urdaneta City University’s Center of Training and Professional Development held the second phase of its Professional Regulation Commission (PRC) accredited CPD program on advanced research writing. The threeday onsite workshop, “Research Writing: Statistical Data Analysis, Results and Discussion, Conclusion, and Recommendation,” provided teachers with an opportunity to earn eight Continuing Professional Development (CPD) units while enhancing their research skills.

The program featured a carefully curated lineup of topics, beginning with statistical data analysis and moving through the critical stages of results and discussion, concluding with sections on crafting effective conclusions and recommendations. Participants were also introduced to essential guidelines for paper presentation and publication, equipping them with skills for broader academic dissemination of their work.

SDG #1: NO POVERTY

Poverty is a multifaceted issue that goes beyond mere lack of income; it encompasses various deprivations in health, education, and living standards. This goal aims to eradicate extreme poverty globally, ensuring that everyone has access to essential resources such as nutritious food, clean water, healthcare, and shelter. Urdaneta City University is committed to reducing poverty by offering scholarships and financial assistance to underprivileged students, ensuring that financial barriers do not prevent talented individuals from pursuing higher education. Additionally, UCU engages in community outreach programs that provide educational workshops and resources to marginalized communities, empowering them with the knowledge and skills necessary for sustainable livelihoods.

UCU CARE PROJECT

The Center for Student Leadership and Development-Character and Values Formation Unit at Urdaneta City University launched an inspiring initiative entitled “UCUIANS CARE PROJECT,” with the theme "Caring Beyond Borders! Healthy Student, Healthy Mind." Aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 1 – No Poverty, this project seeks to promote the values of empathy, generosity, and community-mindedness by encouraging students to practice the timeless principle that caring is sharing.

AFFORDABLE EDUCATION FOR THE BRIGHT FUTURE OF UCU STUDENTS

Urdaneta City University (UCU), through its Center for Student Leadership and Development (CSLD), continues to demonstrate its dedication to fostering an inclusive and equitable learning environment by offering financial assistance through its Tuition Discount Program. This initiative, which aligns with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 1 – No Poverty, is designed to provide essential support to students from economically disadvantaged families, helping reduce the barriers to education and empowering them to achieve their academic goals.

The Tuition Discount Program seeks to alleviate this burden by offering qualifying students reduced tuition fees, allowing them to focus on their studies and personal development without the constant worry of financial hardship.

EXTENSION ACTIVITY: CAS AGAPE

The College of Arts and Sciences, under the leadership of Dean Princess Quintana, organized the EXTENSION ACTIVITY: CAS AGAPE for the students of Tiposu Elementary School on December 15, 2023. This event, which aimed to provide both material and emotional support, was made possible through the collective efforts of the college's faculty and students, who came together to uplift and bring joy to the children in the community.

In terms of objectives, the EXTENSION ACTIVITY: CAS AGAPE Program aimed to accomplish several key goals. Firstly, it focused on providing essential school supplies to underprivileged students, ensuring that they had the necessary tools for their education. This effort helped ease the financial burden on families who might otherwise struggle to afford these basic items. Secondly, the program sought to offer hope and encouragement to children from economically disadvantaged backgrounds, particularly during the holiday season.

CAMPUS STUDENT ORGANIZATIONS’ TRADE FAIR

Urdaneta City University Teams Up with Schools and Barangays to Fight Poverty Through Extension Program

Urdaneta City University (UCU) has officially launched a series of extension programs aimed at addressing poverty in the region. This new initiative, which focuses on empowering local communities through educational support, is a collaborative effort between UCU, local schools, and barangays (villages). The program is part of the university's commitment to supporting the local government’s ongoing efforts to eradicate poverty and contribute to the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goal of ending poverty in all its forms by 2030.

The official launch of the program was marked by the signing of a Memorandum of Agreement led by UCU’s Extension Director, Jerry Dichoso, who played a pivotal role in forging partnerships between the university, local schools, and barangays. The event, held at the respective campuses of the partner schools and barangays, saw the signing of Memoranda of Agreement (MOAs) with several barangay councils and schools in the region, formalizing their commitment to work together in delivering the program’s objectives.

GROWING OPPORTUNITIES: UCU LAUNCHES LIVELIHOOD TRAINING ON MUSHROOM FARMING

Urdaneta City University (UCU) is taking significant steps to support sustainable business initiatives with the launch of a Livelihood Training Program on Mushroom Production, held on September 15, 2019. The program, organized by the Center for Community Development and Extension Services (CCDES) under the leadership of Dr. Mary Ruth Cinchez, aims to provide participants with the practical skills needed to start their own mushroom farming businesses while promoting sustainable agricultural practices.

In partnership with the Provincial Agriculture Office, represented by Mdm. Concordia S. Lee, the training focused on both the technical aspects of mushroom production and the entrepreneurial strategies needed to establish a viable, long-term business. Mdm. Lee, who served as the lead trainer, guided the participants through key processes such as cultivation, harvesting, and the economic potential of mushroom farming as a sustainable business venture.

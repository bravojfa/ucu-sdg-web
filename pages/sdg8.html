Economic growth should benefit everyone, not just a select few. This goal focuses on creating jobs, promoting entrepreneurship, and ensuring fair labor practices that lead to productive employment for all. Urdaneta City University supports career development by organizing job fairs, internships, and networking events that connect students with local businesses. The University also encourages entrepreneurship through workshops and competitions that inspire students to develop innovative ideas and startups, promoting economic growth in the community.

CDA REGION 1 - MOA SIGNING

Urdaneta City University (UCU) has a long-standing mandate to produce graduates who are not only employable but also capable of catalyzing local economic growth. Cooperatives, with their community-owned business model, offer a natural platform for inclusive development, yet the Region 1 cooperative sector still grapples with limited technical expertise and innovation. The Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) is the national body tasked with regulating and nurturing cooperatives. By formalizing a Memorandum of Agreement, UCU gains direct access to CDA’s policy guidance, training resources, and field networks, while CDA secures an academic partner to embed cooperative education in teacher preparation, business programs, and community-extension work.

This projects aims to:

  1. Develop a certificate program in Cooperative Management embedded in UCU’s business curriculum.
  2. Conduct joint faculty-student research on cooperative best practices and sector challenges in Region 1.
  3. Deliver community-based training workshops on financial literacy, governance, and digital tools for local cooperatives.
  4. Establish an annual Cooperative Fair to connect students, cooperatives, and potential investors.
  5. Create a monitoring dashboard that tracks cooperative performance indicators and community impact.

Contributions to the Sustainable Development Goals

The partnership advances SDG 4 (Quality Education) by integrating cooperative studies into formal curricula, SDG 8 (Decent Work & Economic Growth) through entrepreneurship and job creation in member-owned enterprises, and SDG 17 (Partnerships for the Goals) by linking academe, government, and grassroots organizations. Strengthened cooperatives also support SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities & Communities) by anchoring local economies in inclusive, democratic business structures.

General Assessment

Initial stakeholder meetings reveal strong enthusiasm: 14 regional cooperatives have signaled intent to participate in training, and two local government units have pledged venue and logistics support. Baseline data collected by UCU’s Research Office show that only 38 % of surveyed cooperatives keep audited financial statements and 22 % use any form of digital bookkeeping. These gaps underscore the relevance of the planned workshops and certificate program. Key risks include securing sustained funding for outreach activities and managing faculty workload. However, the creation of a joint steering committee and an earmarked seed budget from both institutions mitigates these concerns and positions the MoA for early wins.

Recommendations

Institutionalize a revolving fund—sourced from fair proceeds and modest cooperative membership fees—to underwrite annual training and research grants. This will reduce dependence on one-off project funds and ensure continuity. Pair quantitative metrics (e.g., cooperative survival rates, job creation) with qualitative feedback from members to evaluate impact each year. A balanced scorecard will guide iterative improvements, prevent mission drift, and keep the partnership aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals.

DOLE-RO 1 & UCU for Educational-Based Job Placement Office

The establishment of the Educational-Based Job Placement Office at Urdaneta City University (UCU), in partnership with the Department of Labor and Employment - Regional Office 1 (DOLE-RO 1), marks a significant step toward enhancing the employability of graduating students. This initiative is rooted in the shared goal of bridging the gap between education and employment, ensuring that students are not only academically prepared but also professionally supported in finding relevant job opportunities.

The activity represents a formal collaboration between the university and the labor sector to provide structured support for students through job placements, internships, and career development programs. By aligning academic training with labor market demands, the office aims to produce graduates who are more responsive and competitive in their respective industries. It serves as a centralized platform where students can access resources, guidance, and employment services tailored to their educational background and career goals.

This milestone reinforces UCU's ongoing commitment to holistic student development and DOLE’s mission to promote inclusive employment opportunities. The creation of this office is a proactive response to the evolving needs of the workforce and a testament to the university’s dedication to supporting students beyond the classroom, ultimately preparing them for meaningful and sustainable careers.

This projects aims:

  1. To bridge the gap between education and employment by providing structured job placement services and career support to students and graduates.
  2. To facilitate internships, employment opportunities, and career development programs that align with current industry needs and demands.
  3. To support the professional growth of students by equipping them with the necessary skills, guidance, and resources for successful entry into the workforce.
  4. To strengthen collaboration between the university and labor sector partners in promoting inclusive and accessible employment pathways for future graduates.

Contributions to the Sustainable Development Goals

The establishment of the Educational-Based Job Placement Office at Urdaneta City University, in partnership with DOLE-RO 1, significantly contributes to SDG 4: Quality Education by ensuring that students receive not only academic instruction but also practical guidance on employment and career readiness. By offering job placement services, internship opportunities, and career counseling, the initiative makes education more relevant to real-world demands. It bridges the gap between learning and livelihood, enabling students to apply their knowledge and skills in professional environments after graduation.

This initiative also directly supports SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth by promoting access to productive employment for youth. By aligning educational outputs with labor market needs, the placement office helps graduates secure meaningful jobs that offer fair income and professional development. It aims to reduce youth unemployment and underemployment, which are common issues faced by fresh graduates, by providing direct pathways to employment through partnerships with industries and employers.

Lastly, the program contributes to SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals by strengthening collaboration between the university and government, particularly the Department of Labor and Employment. This partnership reflects a shared commitment to national development goals and highlights the power of joint efforts in solving unemployment and education-to-employment gaps. Through continuous coordination and resource-sharing, the initiative creates a sustainable support system that benefits students, employers, and the broader economy.

General Assessment

The establishment of the Educational-Based Job Placement Office at Urdaneta City University is a highly commendable initiative that addresses a critical need among graduating students — the transition from education to employment. The activity was well-organized and aligned with the university’s mission of producing career-ready graduates. It demonstrated strong institutional commitment to supporting students beyond academic instruction, by offering them access to job placement services and practical career development resources.

One of the strengths of the activity was its strategic partnership with the Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE-RO 1). This collaboration brought credibility, resources, and expertise to the university’s employment facilitation efforts. The presence of labor officials during the launch emphasized the importance of cross-sectoral cooperation in addressing youth unemployment. Additionally, the creation of a dedicated office ensures sustainability and continuity in delivering employment support services to students and alumni.

Overall, the activity met its intended objectives and was received positively by stakeholders. It laid the groundwork for a more structured and institutionalized approach to career development within the university. With continued support, proper staffing, and active engagement with industry partners, the Job Placement Office has the potential to become a key pillar in ensuring that UCU graduates are competitive, confident, and prepared to contribute to national and global development.

Recommendations

To maximize the impact of the Job Placement Office, it is recommended that the university regularly conduct labor market needs assessments and align its academic offerings and career services accordingly. This would help ensure that graduates possess the skills and competencies currently in demand across industries. Additionally, the office should implement digital platforms for job postings, virtual career counseling, and online application systems to make services more accessible to students and alumni.

Another key recommendation is to strengthen engagement with external partners, including local businesses, government agencies, and alumni networks. Building strong relationships with employers can open doors for internships, on-the-job training, and employment opportunities. The office should also consider organizing career development seminars and workshops throughout the academic year, not only for graduating students but also for undergraduates, to build early awareness and preparation for future employment. Regular monitoring and feedback mechanisms should be established to evaluate the effectiveness of services and make continuous improvements.
